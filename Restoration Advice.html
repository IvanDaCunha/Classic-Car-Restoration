What to Look for When Buying a Project Car

Buying a project classic car can be an exciting venture, but it requires careful research, inspection, and planning. Always assess the car's condition, both mechanically and cosmetically, and be sure you have the time, skills, and budget to restore it. The right project car can bring satisfaction and even profit, but it’s important to make an informed decision.

Here are a few things to consider:

  • Check the Frame and Body Condition: Rust can be a project killer. Always check for rust on the frame, floor pans, and the body, especially in hidden areas like the wheel wells and undercarriage.
  • Engine and Transmission: Ensure the engine turns over and that the transmission shifts smoothly. Major engine or transmission work can quickly escalate your costs.
  • Parts Availability: Before committing to a project, ensure the parts needed for the restoration are readily available. Some older models might have rare or hard-to-find parts, making the restoration more costly.
  • Documentation and History: Having the car’s original paperwork, such as the title, maintenance records, and provenance, can help you assess its value and authenticity.
  • Price and Budget: Always compare the asking price with the restoration costs. A car that’s too expensive upfront might not be worth it in the long run if it requires excessive work.
  • Do Your Research: Each classic car model has its own common issues, so be sure to research any known mechanical or cosmetic problems specific to the model you're interested in.
